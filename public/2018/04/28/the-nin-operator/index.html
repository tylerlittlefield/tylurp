The %nin% operator

2018/04/28

I often use the %in% operator when I need to filter a dataset based on multiple values in a single column. For example:

library(tidyverse)

starwars %>% 
  filter(homeworld %in% c("Tatooine", "Naboo")) %>% 
  select(name, homeworld)

## # A tibble: 21 x 2
##    name               homeworld
##    <chr>              <chr>    
##  1 Luke Skywalker     Tatooine

##  3 R2-D2              Naboo

##  5 Owen Lars          Tatooine 
##  6 Beru Whitesun lars Tatooine 
##  7 R5-D4              Tatooine 
##  8 Biggs Darklighter  Tatooine 
##  9 Anakin Skywalker   Tatooine

This operator is so useful because I can avoid doing something like:

starwars %>% 
  filter(homeworld == "Tatooine" | homeworld == "Naboo") %>% 
  select(name, homeworld)

## # A tibble: 21 x 2
##    name               homeworld
##    <chr>              <chr>    
##  1 Luke Skywalker     Tatooine 
##  2 C-3PO              Tatooine 
##  3 R2-D2              Naboo    
##  4 Darth Vader        Tatooine 
##  5 Owen Lars          Tatooine 
##  6 Beru Whitesun lars Tatooine 
##  7 R5-D4              Tatooine 
##  8 Biggs Darklighter  Tatooine 
##  9 Anakin Skywalker   Tatooine 
## 10 Palpatine          Naboo    
## # ... with 11 more rows

However, there is no negation to the %in% operator in base R. So if I want to filter the homeworld column to give me everything except Tatooine and Naboo, I have do something like this:

starwars %>% 
  filter(homeworld != "Tatooine", homeworld != "Naboo") %>% 
  select(name, homeworld)

## # A tibble: 56 x 2
##    name                  homeworld 
##    <chr>                 <chr>     
##  1 Leia Organa           Alderaan  
##  2 Obi-Wan Kenobi        Stewjon   
##  3 Wilhuff Tarkin        Eriadu    
##  4 Chewbacca             Kashyyyk  
##  5 Han Solo              Corellia  
##  6 Greedo                Rodia     
##  7 Jabba Desilijic Tiure Nal Hutta 
##  8 Wedge Antilles        Corellia  
##  9 Jek Tono Porkins      Bestine IV
## 10 Boba Fett             Kamino    
## # ... with 46 more rows

And this is where the Negate function comes in handy. Simply negate the %in% operator to match everything except the elements in a given vector:

'%nin%' <- Negate("%in%")

starwars %>% 
  filter(homeworld %nin% c("Tatooine", "Naboo")) %>% 
  select(name, homeworld)

## # A tibble: 66 x 2
##    name                  homeworld 
##    <chr>                 <chr>     
##  1 Leia Organa           Alderaan  
##  2 Obi-Wan Kenobi        Stewjon   
##  3 Wilhuff Tarkin        Eriadu    
##  4 Chewbacca             Kashyyyk  
##  5 Han Solo              Corellia  
##  6 Greedo                Rodia     
##  7 Jabba Desilijic Tiure Nal Hutta 
##  8 Wedge Antilles        Corellia  
##  9 Jek Tono Porkins      Bestine IV
## 10 Yoda                  <NA>      
## # ... with 56 more rows
